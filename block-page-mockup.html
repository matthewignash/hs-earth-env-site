📖 Story

Why this block matters

Connecting today to the unit's anchor question: How do you build for a planet that occasionally tries to destroy what you've built?

Yesterday you met the four boundary types. Today we go deeper on the two that account for most coastal-city hazards on Earth — subduction zones (Tōhoku, Cascadia, Sumatra) and transform faults (Anatolian, San Andreas). The same physics produces the same hazards everywhere a boundary type exists. By the end of class you'll be able to look at any city on a coast and predict, with reasonable confidence, what its dominant hazards are.

You'll also get your first look at the Tectonic City Builder simulator — the tool you'll use across Blocks 4-12 to design and defend a city plan under real tectonic constraints.
